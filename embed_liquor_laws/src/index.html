Local liquor laws

Local cities, towns and even localities are allowed to vote on liquor laws in their communities. Some are wet, some are dry, but most are mixed. Use the chart below to search communities and see what the laws are like in your area. Click on column headers to view law definitions.

B-On: Sale of beer for on premises consumption authorized.

B-Off: Sale of beer for off-premises consumption authorized.

W-On: Sale of wine for on-premises consumption authorized.

W-Off: Sale of wine for off-premises consumption authorized.

DS-Off: Sale of distilled spirits for off-premises consumption authorized.

MB: Sale of mixed beverages authorized. Not used to describe areas where sale of mixed beverages only authorized in restaurants.

RM: Sale of mixed beverages authorized but only in restaurants.

Wet

The entire jurisdiction, every square inch of it, is wet for that type of sale.

Partly Wet

There are one or more parts of the jurisdiction in which a particular type of beverage sale is legal but there are other parts in that jurisdiction where that type of sale is not legal.

Dry

The jurisdiction is dry throughout. No type of alcoholic beverage sales is permitted.

Combine, part of Grapevine and Ovilla are dry areas.

Source: Texas Alcoholic Beverage Commission
